42 Gulf Stream Index

Description: The monthly Gulf Stream North Wall Index presented here are based on the gridded EN.4.2.2 analyses dataset from 1954 to 2022 (https://www.metoffice.gov.uk/hadobs/en4/), calculated following [68].

Indicator family:

Contributor(s): Zhuomin Chen

Affiliations: ?

42.1 Introduction to Indicator

The T200-based Gulf Stream Index is calculated as the standardized first principal component time series from the empirical orthogonal function (EOF) analysis of the 200 m temperature time series at the 20 base points (selected along the climatological 15°C isotherm at 200 m between 74° and 55°W) and it represents the meridional fluctuation of the Gulf Stream North Wall [69,70].

42.2 Key Results and Visualizations

The Gulf Stream Index suggest that recent years (2021-2022) the GS almost maintains its relative northward shift relative to the long-term mean.

42.4 Implications

The Gulf Stream North Wall Index indicates the meridional shift of the Gulf Stream position on monthly timescale, which may affect the slope water properties intruding onto the continental shelf. The GSNW index is also suggested to be an good indicator for biomass distribution of multiple marine fishes (e.g., silver hake [71]). As the Gulf Stream has become less stable and shifted northward in the last decade (Fig. ), warmer ocean temperatures have been observed on the northeast shelf [72], and a higher proportion of Warm Slope Water has been present in the Gulf of Maine Northeast Channel [73], and sea surface height along the U.S. east coast has increased. Since 2008, the Gulf Stream has moved closer to the Grand Banks, reducing the supply of cold, fresh, and oxygen-rich Labrador Current waters to the Northwest Atlantic Shelf [74]
